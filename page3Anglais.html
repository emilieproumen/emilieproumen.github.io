Émilie Proumen

eco-designed kettle

type

eco-design of a household appliance product

year

2023

software

SimaPro

As part of this project, we chose a household appliance in order to eco-design it. The chosen product is a Silvercrest electric kettle. The first step was performing a life cycle analysis. To do this, we studied the different usage scenarios and decided which one was the most representative in order to deduce a functional unit and a reference flow. We then carried out measurements of electricity consumption in typical cases of use. Then we dismantled the object in order to take an inventory of all the parts, weigh them and determine their compositions. Once we had our data, we modeled the kettle using Simapro software. This step allowed us to identify the major sources of environmental impact, and what levers we were going to work on. We therefore proposed three solutions (partial, realistic, ambitious) adapted to this observation. We modified the number of parts, the materials, but above all looked for a solution to avoid the biggest negative impact: excess electricity consumption. We therefore thought about a proposal for a retractable kettle, adaptable to the quantity of water required for use (a cup for tea, a liter for pasta), in order to compensate for excess electrical consumption.
