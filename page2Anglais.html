As part of the TN20 subject, I modeled an oscillating fan in 3D on Catia V5 software. For this project, I first disassembled the fan to understand how it worked. So I discovered the gears of the motor that turn the propeller, as well as the four-bar mechanism driving the different oscillations. The second step was to model each part, starting with the volume, then moving on to the surface, in particular for the foot, the propeller or the casing. I then made a positioning skeleton, on which I was able to carry out my assembly. This makes my modeling more solid, since each sub-assembly or part only depends on the skeleton. I drew this with the main axes and planes, on which I then constrained the sub-assemblies and parts. This method also allowed me to configure it (it is possible to change the angle of inclination of the casing in relation to the foot).
